    [ Main Building - Section: I Backtrack ]

    • Go Back To Passage Way From New Building That Leads Back To Main Building, Section 1.
    • Automatically Using The Spirit Bell Item You Can Now Go Back There.
    • 1F - Starting Area/Foyer. Examine The Furniture Piece For Cleaning The Chalkboard Erasers
      "It Is Where You Saw Seol Ji-hyeon Leaning Down,
      And Looking At One Of The Tree Ghosts Plant / Eggs Locations Earlier In The Game".
      Look Underneath The Furniture Piece To Open A Secret Compartment
      Using The Wooden Handle Item. Obtaining Ghost Story #3 Boy, Kills Girl.
    • 1F - Workshop. Combine Yellow And Red Key
      To Open The "Box For Counseling" At Classroom 2-2 To Get Morse Code Chart.
    • Dissemble This Key Then Combine The Red And Blue Key
      To Unlock "Box For Counseling" In Front Of Classroom 2-5 To Get Small Key Dark.
    • Dissemble This Key Then Combine The Dark And Yellow Key
      To Unlock The "Box For Opinions"
      In Front Of The Workshop
      To Get Ghost Story #6 Wailing From The Art Room.
    • Finally Dissemble This Key And Then Combine
      Dark And Blue Key To Open The "Box For Opinions"
      In Front Of Gym Storage Room To Find Final Paper Origami Crane.
    • 2F - Classroom 2-7. Place All 5 "Piece Of Class Schedule (Mathematics)"
      Onto The Schedule To Obtain Ghost Story #11 Incorrectly Solved Math Problem.

    [ Main Building - Section: II Backtrack ]

    • Last Backtrack Area Before Returning To New Building Again.
    • 1F - Faculty Office 1. Rearrange Portraits In This Room Based On Your Screenshot
      In Chairman's Office To Obtain Ghost Story #20 Missing Children.
      Answer Phone Again For Easter Egg.
    • 1F - Nurses Office. You Can Now Use The Two Lenses
      To Find The Code For The Safe In The Chairman's Office Of The New Building.
      Look For Circled Numbers And Symbols And Go Line By Line.
      You Are Looking For 4 Digit Code, Recommend To Take Screenshots.
    • 2F - Faculty Office 2. Place Typewriter Keys Onto Typewriter.
    • Use "Dying Message" Document As A Clue
      To Open The Locked Box In The Student Department Office On 2F.
    • Use Small Master Key To Open The Typewriter Desk
      To Obtain Ghost Story #1 Ghost Of A Houseparent.
    • 3F - Music Supplies Room. Use Morse Code Chart On The Metronome
      Here To Get The Code For Music Appreciation Room's Locked Box.
    • A Quick Pendulum Movement Represents A Dot
      While A Slow Movement Represents A Dash.
      Everytime The Pendulum Reaches Either End It Makes A Click Noise.
      When You Start The Pendulum Movement Wait Until The Pendulum
      Goes To The Left First And Clicks Then Start Reading It From There.

    [ New Building - Final Tasks ]

    • 1F - History Room. Solve "Paper Origami Crane" Puzzle On The Frame
      In History Room Using The 5 Paper Origami Cranes
      You Have Found To Obtain Ghost Story #8 Extreme Dieting Death.
    • 1F - Chairman's Office. Unlock Safe With Code From 1F - Nurses Office
      To Obtain Ghost Story #2 Uncrossable Bridge.
    • 1F - Music Appreciation Room. Unlock The Locked Box With The Passcode Obtained
      From The Metronome Puzzle To Obtain Ghost Story #9 Lost Face.
    • There Are A Few Areas/Ghosts Left In The Last Sections Of The Game.
      The Auditorium/Gym And The Labyrinth Which Are All Self Contained.
      You Should Just Be Missing The No Pain, No Gain Achievement Now.

[ Is This A Guide Or Walkthrough? ]


~ Not exactly. This Checklist will help you obtain every
Miscellaneous Item, Ghost / Boss and Ghost Stories.
As well as a few other things, such as the Doodles to unlock a new character for you to play as.
Other Items that are not listed you will obatin natrually as you progress and search.
Like Healing Items and the Coins to buy them.
This Checklist leaves those things as well as some puzzle solutions for you to discover and solve.
So search rooms as you would without this Checklist.

~ There are 10 Endings in this game. 2 of which are new additions in the PC Remake.
You will need to discover the new character to get endings #9 and #10.
The ending you get is based on how you interact with characters in dialogue trees.
Also, some other little sutble things as well.
Here is a link to good Guide explaining how to get all the 10 Endings.
This Checklist does not lead you to a certain ending if curious.
If this is your first time playing White Day.
You may want to consider playing on "Normal" mode first to give you a feel for the game,
as some may find it very difficult. Then with some familiarity and desire to 100% the game.
Go back through on "Hard" mode and use this Checklist to help.
You can still use this Checklist for "Normal" mode, just not everything will apply.
A way to consider "Normal" mode vs "Hard" mode besides AI adjustments.
On "Normal" mode you get to read about most of the Ghosts.
On "Hard" mode you get to encounter the Ghosts.
More scary right? White Day is not a super long game once you know what to do.

[ How To Use This Checklist ]


~ The Checklist is based on the Remake on PC of White Day and not the Original game.
As well as playing on "Hard" mode, because you can't get
100% of everything on "Normal" mode or below. 100% in regards to this Checklists purposes.
To get actual 100% you are going to need to play "Hell" mode as well.
To collect things such as Trophy Items that are only available on that difficulty.
You have to beat "Hard" mode to unlock "Hell" mode.
Even between PC and Mobile versions of White Day: Remake there are differences.

~ A good way to use this Checklist is when you are ready to leave a certain area.
Reference it then so you don't ruin the game for yourself on your first playthrough.
Remember, Puzzle solutions and the way forward to progress
is explained to you in the multitude of Documents you get in the game.

~ The sorting method for the Checklist may need some
adjustments to be the most effective and efficient.
So please, as stated above, drop me an email if it can be improved.
It isn't designed for a speedrun or anything.
It is setup based on ( Buildings > Floors > Rooms ) you have access to.
There is a certain degree of backtracking needed in White Day regardless.

~ The six buttons you see on the ( Playthrough ) page.
Clicking them so the Eye Symbol is crossed out will hide that category in the Checklist.
Helpful to simplify the Checklist if you only need one category visible.

~ The ( Other ) category pertains to directions or explanations.
Usually not directly with obtaining an item.

~ Use the Searchbar provided within the Checklist to search for keywords.
Such as Pin, Weight, 3F, ect. to quickly filter the Checklist.

~ There are a total of ( 20 Ghosts ) to encounter and ( 20 Ghost Stories ) explaning their backstory.
For this Checklists purposes, I have seperated ( Ghosts ) to 14 total and
put the other 6 of them into the ( Bosses ) category.
My reasoning is for those 6 ( Ghosts ), they are more than just a
jumpscare and there is a little more to them.
Being able to kill you or you being able to kill them for example, so I qualifed them as ( Bosses ).
If you add ( Ghosts ) and ( Bosses ) categories together you will get
a total of 22 and not the expected 20.
This is because the Lying Female Student ghost is listed 3 times in the Checklist.
You encounter her 3 seperate times in different locations
but she only counts as 1 ( Ghost ) in the total of 20.
